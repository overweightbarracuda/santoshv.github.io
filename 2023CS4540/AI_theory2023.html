Fall 2023. MW: 9:30-10:45, Mason 5134

Santosh Vempala, Klaus 2222, Office hour: TBA

What is the basis of intelligence? How can neural networks, artificial or natural, provably lead to perception, learning and cognition? We'll begin by studying a few classics, highlight the gaps and challenges posed by modern artificial models and the brain, and then discuss more recent rigorous proposals.

Prerequisites: algorithms, linear algebra.


Grading:
HW: 4-6 Problem sets.
Exams: Two in-class exams. No final.
Project: Each team can choose one of the following options: (a) read a paper and provide an insightful explanation/simpler proof/ generalization (b) prove a conjecture (c) formulate a precise conjecture supported by experimental or theoretical evidence. A list of candidate projects will be provided.
